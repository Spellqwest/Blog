# Introduction

For our project in the *Games and Gaming* course, our group is developing a small game prototype called **Spellqwest**.
*Spellqwest* is a **roguelike typing game** where the player casts spells by typing words on their keyboard. The central idea is to combine **typing mechanics** with **movement and combat**, turning the keyboard itself into the game’s battlefield.
In the game, the player takes on the role of **Qwentin**, a retired scriptomancer. Scriptomancers are mages who use written language as their source of magic. Instead of casting spells through buttons or menus, Qwentin writes them into existence by typing the correct words.


## Core Gameplay

The game is structured around two main gameplay layers: **map progression** and **combat encounters**.
Between encounters, the player moves across a **map with branching paths**, choosing which nodes to visit next. Each node represents a different type of encounter, such as combat, rewards, shops or special events. At the end of each stage, the player faces a **boss encounter** before progressing further.
When entering combat, the player is placed in a battlefield that is **laid out like a keyboard**.
Each key on the keyboard represents a position on the battlefield. When the player presses a key to type a letter, the character **moves to the corresponding key on the battlefield**. For example, pressing **A** moves the player to the position representing the **A key**.
At the same time, the letters the player types form **spell words**. Completing a word casts the corresponding spell. This means that **movement and spellcasting happen simultaneously**: typing a spell not only triggers magic, but also determines how the player moves across the battlefield.
Enemies approach the player while they type, so the player must balance **positioning, typing speed, and accuracy** in order to survive.


## Spells

Different spells produce different effects once their word is completed. These can include:

- **Projectile spells** that travel across the battlefield  
- **Area-of-effect spells** that damage multiple enemies  
- **Other spell types** that expand the player’s options during combat  

Because the player must physically move across the keyboard to type a word, choosing which spell to cast also affects **where the player moves during combat**.


## Progression and Rewards

After encounters, players receive rewards that help them progress through the run. These rewards can include:

- **New spells**, expanding the available spell vocabulary  
- **Items** that modify gameplay or spell effects  
- **Coins**, which can be used for upgrades or purchases  

Since the game follows a roguelike structure, each run will present different choices and combinations of rewards.


## Theme and Setting

The world of *Spellqwest* is loosely themed around **language and writing**, but also places an emphasis on *puns and humor*. 
Early concepts for areas include places such as the **Text Plains** or the **Palindome** but there are plenty other plays on words in the works.
These ideas are still evolving as the project develops, but they help frame the concept of a world where **words themselves have magical power**.
